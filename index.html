About

My name is Enrique Garcia Perez. I'm a software developer at the SAP Innovation Center Network in Potsdam, Germany. Since I started working at the SAP I have contributed to various projects related to Source Code Analysis, In-House Entrepreneurship and Healthcare. In February 2014 I started working on a product called Medical Research Insights, which focuses on providing tailored analytics to the needs of physicians.

My education started in the German School Colegio Humboldt in my home town Puebla, Mexico. I attained a B.Eng. in Computer Systems Engineering from the Universidad de las Americas in Puebla, Mexico. During my undergraduate studies participated in an exchange program with the Technical University Munich in Germany where I did several courses and also wrote my Bachelor's thesis with title Autonomous Distributed Control and Management via MAPE

I attained a M.Sc. in Computer Science from the Technical University Munich, Ludwig Maximillian Univestiy and Augsburg University. By the end of my master studies I joined the MIT AgeLab where I wrote my master thesis. For this research task, we applied classification algorithms to detect the cognitive workload level that a person has while driving a car. This work was done under the supervision of Bryan Reimer and Bruce Mehler.

My hobbies include traveling, photography, and sports like Crossfit, biking, swimming and running. In the past few months I have also tried freeletics, that is very similar to Crossfit, but using only your own body weight.

tvoicer

Voices to the people

tvoicer is a audio message social network to help people share their voice with the world. The idea of tvoicer is provide an easy way for people to share their thought and emotions using their own voice. With tvoicer you can record a 5 seconds audio snippet and share it to all other tvoicer users. It is also possible to share your tvoices over facebook and twitter.

The technological stack used to build tvoicer includes the google cloud platform to store and serve tvoices. The backend is written in java and currently exists native applications for iOS and Android devices. As a co-founder and developer I was mainly in charge of building the iOS application.

Cognitive Workload Detection

Master Thesis
Title: A Symbolic Approach for Classification of Cognitive Workload in Drivers

Abstract
Cognitive workload estimation in drivers has important implication for traffic safety and the optimization of in-vehicle information systems design. This thesis proposes a state detection system based on the use of physiological signals i.e. electrocardiogram and electrodermal activity because they are non-intrusive and non-invasive. Further- more, the streaming data from the physiological signals is transformed into a symbolic representation to benefit from the time series component of the data and reduce its dimensionality. Then, a machine learning approach uses a similarity measure to dis- criminate the driver’s cognitive workload levels. An alternative symbolic representation that could potentially allow the system to perform real time classification was also evaluated.

The classification system was evaluated using physiology data collected from 99 subjects driving in a naturalistic environment. An instrumented vehicle (AwareCar) was used to collect the data. During the driving period, the subjects performed secondary tasks “n-back” to induce different cognitive workload levels. Experimental results of discriminating the highest level of cognitive workload (2-back) from the lowest level (just driving) yield a 82% accuracy with the most simple classification approach and 77% using an approach able to classify cognitive workload in real time. The analysis conducted in this thesis shows that the structure of the physiology measures is similar across participants. This thesis lays the foundation for using symbolic representations of time series data to assess a driver’s cognitive workload level in a real time monitoring system.

Autonomous Distributed Control and Management via MAPE

Bachelor Thesis

At the heart of an autonomic system is a control system, which is a combination of components that act together to maintain actual system attribute values close to desired specifications.

An autonomic system consists of a set of autonomic elements that contain and manage resources and deliver services to humans or other autonomic elements. An autonomic element consists of one autonomic manager and one or more managed elements. The core of an autonomic element is a control loop that integrates the manager with the managed element.

The Monitor Analyze Plan Execute (MAPE) control loop is our proposal to structure the functionality needed on autonomously behaving nodes. The MAPE loop runs on every controllable node inside the Autonomous Control and Management Platform (ACMP). It decides which configuration changes are being reflected on the managed hardware or software device. This project was done as part of the AutHoNe - Autonomic Networking project.
